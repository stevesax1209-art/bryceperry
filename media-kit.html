Bryce Perry is a Parkinson's disease advocate, award-winning podcaster, keynote speaker, health technology founder, and community builder who has transformed a devastating diagnosis into a global platform for hope, connection, and change.


When Bryce was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease in 2010 at the age of 40, he received a pamphlet, a prescription, and very little else. What he didn't find was a community that reflected his reality ‚Äî an active person, completely unprepared for the challenges ahead. Rather than accept that absence, Bryce built what was missing. What started as a small online support group grew into Doing Life Today (DLT), a thriving multi-channel platform that today serves more than 100,000 patients, caregivers, and advocates across the globe ‚Äî built in just one year.


As the host of two award-winning podcasts ‚Äî the Living with Parkinson's Podcast (127+ episodes) and Life in Motion ‚Äî Bryce has become one of the most recognized and trusted voices in the Parkinson's community. His shows have featured neurologists, researchers, advocates, and patients in candid conversations that consistently top the charts in health and wellness programming. His media work has been featured on CBC Radio, Global News, Parkinson's Life Magazine, and numerous health publications.


Driven by his belief that patients should have a voice in the tools designed for them, Bryce founded the Parkinson's Community Network (PCN) App ‚Äî a patient-centered mobile tool offering medication tracking, symptom logging, community connection, and AI-assisted support. The app has been embraced by thousands of users and has become a model for patient-informed health technology development.


On stage, Bryce is equal parts storyteller, educator, and motivator. His keynotes ‚Äî delivered at venues ranging from small community health centers to international medical congresses ‚Äî consistently earn the highest attendee ratings of any speaker at the events he joins. His most requested talk, Welcome to the Club That Nobody Wanted to Join, consistently receives standing ovations at Parkinson's events worldwide.
